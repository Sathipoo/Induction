🌿 It Begins With Rich Milk – and Turns Into Buttermilk

We start with high-quality milk from well-cared-for cows, collected fresh every day. But here’s the beautiful twist — we don’t just churn cream.

We take that milk and cultivate fresh curd first — the same kind your grandmother would set overnight.
Then we churn this curd into buttermilk, using a traditional wooden or slow churning method.

This buttermilk is light, aromatic, and probiotic-rich — the perfect base for cultured ghee, which is known to be more digestible and deeply nourishing.

🧈 From Buttermilk to White Butter

As we churn, fresh white butter begins to rise — soft, smooth, and full of vitality.

We carefully collect this white butter by hand.
This isn’t machine-processed butter. It’s alive. It smells like childhood.
And this is what becomes your golden ghee.

🔥 Slow-Cooked, Just Like the Old Days

We then heat the white butter slowly over a low flame. No rushing. No high heat. Just the old-fashioned way — letting the ghee clarify naturally.

As the butter melts, you’ll see the transformation:

  • First it bubbles,
  • Then it turns clear,
  • And finally, golden.

The milk solids settle at the bottom, giving the ghee its nutty aroma — the one that fills your kitchen and your heart.

We strain the ghee by hand, and while it’s still warm, we bottle it fresh — with all its richness, purity, and flavor intact.

Pure Buffalo Ghee

🐃 Arshith Fresh Buffalo Ghee — The Richness That Feeds Generations

Some flavors are bold. Some aromas linger longer. Some memories are built around one strong spoon of ghee — the kind that sticks to your fingers, fills your senses, and feeds your strength.

That's Buffalo Ghee.

At Arshith Fresh, we make Buffalo Ghee the way it's always been made in the heartlands — with thick curd, traditional churning, patient cooking, and respect for the animal that gives it.

🌾 It Starts With Full-Fat Milk — From Native Buffaloes

We source our milk from well-nourished native buffaloes, cared for by local farmers. Buffalo milk is naturally richer than cow milk — with:

  • Higher fat content
  • More cream per litre
  • A naturally white, dense texture that makes the ghee thick and aromatic

We let the milk settle, boil it gently, and then set curd overnight, just like our elders did. From there, we churn it into thick, white butter using a wooden bilona (or slow churning method).

🔥 Slow Cooked to Gold — With No Rush, No Refining

The butter is then heated slowly over a low flame, in wide-bottomed iron or clay pans. As it melts, the ghee transforms gradually:

  • First it bubbles
  • Then it begins to deepen in aroma
  • And finally, it turns golden cream, while milk solids settle at the bottom

We don't filter it with machines. We strain it by hand, while still warm, and bottle it fresh — without preservatives, palm oil, or shortcuts.

❌ What We Never Add

  • ❌ No color
  • ❌ No preservatives
  • ❌ No chemical essence
  • ❌ No hydrogenated fats
  • ❌ No blending with cow ghee or vanaspati

Just pure, heritage-grade buffalo ghee — made in small batches, by people who know what real ghee should taste like.

🍛 Why Buffalo Ghee Is So Special

Buffalo ghee is known for its:

  • Thicker consistency

  • Rich, deep aroma that brings out flavor in festive foods and sweets

It's best for:

  • Deep frying
  • Preparing traditional sweets like Mysore Pak, Badusha, or Kova
  • Adding richness to gravies, parathas, and biryani
  • Making herbal oils or Ayurvedic massage balms (Abhyanga)

Natural Honey

🍯 Arshith Fresh Natural Honey — Straight From the Hive, Just Like Nature Meant It

Not every sweetness is sugar. Some come with the hum of bees, the scent of wildflowers, and the quiet work of time.

That's honey.

And at Arshith Fresh, our honey isn't factory-processed or sugar-thickened. It's raw, real, and respectfully harvested — just like our ancestors took it from the forest: one drop at a time, one bloom at a time.

🐝 How Our Honey Begins — With Flowers, Forests & Free Bees

We don't raise bees in boxes. We let them roam wild and free in the natural forests and herbal landscapes of India — buzzing through neem blossoms, jamun trees, tulsi patches, mustard fields, and more.

Bees naturally gather nectar from a variety of flowers — not monoculture farms — which makes our honey:

  • Multifloral
  • Seasonal
  • Richer in flavor, aroma, and medicinal value

We let them build their hives on their own — no chemical stimulants, no forced feeding, no synthetic wax.

🍯 From Hive to Home — Without Machines or Middlemen

Once the honeycombs are full:

  • We harvest the honey only when the bees have capped the honey (a natural sign of maturity)
  • We cut the combs by hand, press them gently using wooden or stainless-steel tools
  • Then we filter it once — just enough to remove wax bits and bee particles
  • And we bottle it raw — no heating, no pasteurizing, no refining

What you get is cloudy, thick, golden-brown honey — full of pollen, enzymes, flavor, and healing.

No sugar syrup.

No artificial enzymes.

No plastic bottling.

Just nature in its sweetest form.
